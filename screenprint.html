P2: Is Race Biologically Real? (Part 1)

Race is not biologically real.

EVIDENCE

In science class, we swabbed our cheeks to get our DNA and used a PCR machine to make copies of it so we could see it on a gel. Our data from this year showed that kids from completely different backgrounds had the exact same matching DNA patterns. We also did a quick class survey of traits like rolling your tongue or ear shape, and those did not group by race at all. Plus, world maps show that things like skin color change slowly from place to place based on the sun, not big racial borders.

REASONING, PART ONE

The biological idea of race means thinking that humans are split into completely different groups that are totally different on the inside, and that you can tell a person's group just by looking at their skin color or hair. In our lab, the PCR machine just helped us make copies of a tiny piece of DNA so we could physically see how alike we all are.

REASONING, PART TWO

Our lab evidence shows that race is not written in our DNA. If race were biologically real, then kids of different races would have had completely separate DNA patterns in our test with no matches. But because our DNA bands matched up across the whole class, it proves humans are mostly identical on the inside.

My 21st Century Skill

I used Critical Thinking here because I had to look at real data and maps instead of just guessing. It took a lot of focus to load the tiny DNA samples into the gel lanes without messing up.

P3: Is Race Socially Real? (Part 2)

Race is socially and psychologically real.

EVIDENCE

First, we looked at the Implicit Association Test. This is a fast computer game where you sort faces and words. It measures how fast your brain matches good or bad words with different groups of people. It shows that most Americans have hidden biases they do not even know about. Second, we watched a video where a teacher treated kids differently just because of their eye color. The group told they were bad got sad and did worse on their work, while the good group started acting mean.

REASONING, PART ONE

For something to be socially real, it means it is real because of how people act. This happens because of implicit bias, which is a trick our brain plays without us knowing. It creates cultural racism, which is like smog in the air that we all breathe in from TV and movies, and systemic racism, which is like a moving walkway that pushes everyone in one direction unless you actively turn around and walk away.

REASONING, PART TWO

I know race is socially real because it changes how people live their lives. The computer test shows our brains are trained by the world around us, and the eye-color video shows how fast people change when they are put into groups. If race did not matter socially, everyone would score the same on the computer test and the kids in the video would have just ignored the teacher.
